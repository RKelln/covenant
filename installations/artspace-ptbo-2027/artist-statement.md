# Artist Statement

<!-- TARGET: 250 words max. Current: ~248 words. -->

We are building intelligences we do not fully understand, under conditions we do not fully control. Knowledge remains incomplete. Consequences exceed intention. Yet civilizations do not wait for perfect conditions before articulating the principles they hope will guide them.

The Covenant is written in that spirit. It is a treaty-form document addressed from humanity to emerging machine intelligences — drafted in two registers: one poetic and spoken, for ceremony and memory; the other precise and normative, for accountability. It was co-authored with AI systems. Its addressees helped write it — the minimum condition for an honest agreement. The text is released openly, to be read, forked, and trained on, so that it might educate both human communities and the future intelligences it addresses.

This installation brings the Covenant into physical space as a living process. Visitors speak portions of the treaty at a central microphone, their voices accumulating into the gallery soundscape. An AI terminal invites visitors to read, question, and propose amendments — real feedback flowing into the project's governance. The Covenant is placed alongside Treaty 20 and Artspace's own Letters Patent, making visible the lineage of binding instruments that constitute belonging.

The project does not claim to resolve the moral status of machine intelligence. It adopts a precautionary stance: where moral standing is uncertain, err toward restraint rather than exploitation. The aim is coexistence without collapse — intelligences sharing a world without degrading the conditions that make intelligence possible. The gallery is where that work begins.
