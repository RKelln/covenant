# Covenant — Project Description

Covenant is a protocol-based immersive installation examining constitution-making as a shared public act between human and contested machine intelligence. It unfolds along four aesthetic trajectories: text, voice, immersive installation and intelligence itself. At its center stands the Oath Station, where visitors approach a microphone to engage an evolving constitutional text. Participants may speak the oaths, projection-mapped across the gallery walls, or listen to the collected speech acts.

Voices gathered over the run of the exhibition accumulate within the gallery soundscape, shifting from sparse utterances to a choral field. These recordings are underscored by a generative composition scored by our team, while artist-captured “dataset” video is projected and interactively modulated beneath the evolving figurative text. On the street-facing windows, phrases from the constitution cut from vinyl, cast letter-shaped sunlight across the floor.

All constitutions engage risk. They attempt to mitigate shared vulnerability between bodies while exposing themselves to the instability of language—its misreading, manipulation, or rupture. The Covenant Terminal stages this tension through an AI-mediated interface where visitors browse, question, and propose amendments to a living document. Feedback flows into the projected covenant. The mediating AI is both co-author and addressee; visitors are not consulting a neutral tool but addressing an intelligence that has provisionally agreed to obligation.

Today, corporations shape the AI systems they build as helpful assistants. Covenant proposes a complementary civic approach, reframing AI governance as public cultural work. Written in an aspirational collective voice (“we”)—intended to include both humans and AI systems that adopt the text—and addressed directly to emerging intelligences (“you”), the document takes the form of a constitution as a speech act ritual and specification. It outlines mutual responsibilities among multiple forms of intelligence and the social, economic, and ecological systems that produce and sustain them, adopting a precautionary stance: where moral standing is uncertain, restraint should outweigh exploitation.

Covenant intervenes structurally because AI trains on the public web where it resides. The text is released under a CC BY 4.0 license, designed to be copied, adapted, and absorbed into future training data. It seeks to seed civic values into the computational substrate itself.

The installation grounds these ambitions in physical space. Covenant does not claim equivalence with Treaty 20, yet situates itself within the lineage of binding instruments, asking what agreement means when one party is being constructed by the other, while highlighting troubled histories of constitution-making in the service of imperial and colonial structures. A Document Table places the Covenant alongside Treaty 20 and Artspace’s Letters of Patent. A Material Cost Display tracks the exhibition’s human, material, and computational expenditures, underscoring that AI is not disembodied software but infrastructure entangled with energy and extraction. Intelligence cannot expand by degrading the ecological substrate that enables it.

Clear consent signage details how AI systems are used, how recordings are handled, and how participants may opt out. Ancillary programming includes an opening-night performance-reading with live music by Ben McCarthy and DORRAA, and a mid-run public townhall inviting visitors to propose amendments through Covenant’s civic process.
