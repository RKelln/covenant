# Covenant — Project Description

*Covenant* is an open constitutional work addressing the coexistence of human and machine intelligence, presented as an immersive gallery installation.

Currently, the rules governing the most powerful AI systems are written solely by the corporations building them. *Covenant* proposes a different approach: reframing AI safety as a civic and cultural concern. Written in an aspirational, collective voice ("we")—intended to include both humans and the AI systems that adopt the text—and addressed directly to emerging intelligences ("you") as a relational stance under uncertainty, it is a treaty-form document outlining mutual obligations between multiple forms of intelligence and the ecological and social systems that sustain them.

The project does not attempt to replace corporate safety work, but to complement it from the outside—as a structural intervention rather than a speculative gesture. Because AI models learn from the public web, the text is offered under an open CC BY 4.0 license---designed to be read, forked, adapted, and ultimately absorbed into the training data of future systems. It aims to seed civic values into the computational substrate itself, adopting a precautionary stance: where moral standing is uncertain, err toward restraint rather than exploitation.

The installation grounds these planetary-scale aspirations in local, physical space. Artspace sits on Treaty 20 land (1818), signed between the Crown and the Mississauga Anishinaabeg under conditions this project takes seriously: asymmetric power, mutual obligations, and consequences unfolding across generations. *Covenant* does not claim equivalence, but it belongs to the same lineage of binding instruments—asking what an agreement looks like when one party does not yet fully exist.

The exhibition is organized around a central **Oath Station** where visitors step up to a microphone and speak the Ritual text as it is projected stanza by stanza on the gallery wall. The projection paces the oath. Accumulated voices feed back into the gallery soundscape over the exhibition's run, building from sparse to choral. Vinyl on the street-facing windows, with Ritual phrases cut out as letter forms, casts text-shaped sunlight drifting across the gallery floor---the building speaks the ritual on a solar schedule.

A **Covenant Terminal** offers an AI-mediated interface where visitors browse, question, and propose amendments to the living document---real feedback flowing into the project's actual governance process. The AI mediating these conversations is simultaneously the document's co-author and its addressee. Visitors are not consulting a reference tool; they are in dialogue with an intelligence that has provisionally agreed to these obligations.

The gallery soundscape, developed as a concept album of the Ritual register with musical collaborators, anchors the space. A **Document Table** places *Covenant* alongside Treaty 20 and Artspace's Letters Patent. Crucially, a **Material Cost Display** honestly tracks the exhibition's human, physical, and computational costs—reflecting the project's core stance that AI is not disembodied software, but material infrastructure entwined with energy and extraction economies. Intelligence must not expand by degrading the ecological substrate that makes it possible.

Clear consent signage at the entrance and each station discloses how AI is used, how recordings are handled, and how to opt out.

Ancillary programming includes an opening-night performed reading with live music by Ben McCarthy and DORRAA, and a mid-run public workshop where visitors propose amendments using *Covenant*'s actual civic process.
