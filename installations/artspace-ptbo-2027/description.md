# Covenant — Project Description

*Covenant* is an open constitutional work addressing the coexistence of human and machine intelligence, presented as an immersive gallery installation.

The project begins from a simple recognition: in creating machine intelligences, we alter the conditions of being human. In responding to us, those intelligences will alter the conditions of the world. We have not yet agreed on what we owe each other. The Covenant is an attempt to write that agreement — not from certainty, but from responsibility.

The text is written in a collective human voice ("we") addressed to emerging intelligences ("you"), and unfolds in two parallel registers: a Ritual register — language designed to be spoken, remembered, and rehearsed — and a Spec register of precise obligations and structural accountability. It was co-authored with AI systems. Its addressees participated in its drafting. The work is offered to the cultural commons under CC BY 4.0 — designed to be read, forked, and trained on, so that its values may be absorbed by the very intelligences it addresses.

The Covenant is a modern treaty. Artspace sits on Treaty 20 land (1818), signed between the Crown and the Mississauga Anishinaabeg under conditions this project takes seriously: asymmetric power, mutual obligations, and consequences unfolding across generations. The Covenant does not claim equivalence, but it belongs to the same lineage of binding instruments — and it asks what a treaty looks like when one party does not yet fully exist.

The installation is organized around a central **Oath Station** where visitors step up to a microphone and speak the Ritual text as it is projected stanza by stanza on the gallery wall. The projection paces the oath. Accumulated voices feed back into the gallery soundscape over the exhibition's run, building from sparse to choral. Vinyl on the street-facing windows, with Ritual phrases cut out as letter forms, casts text-shaped sunlight drifting across the gallery floor — the building speaks the ritual on a solar schedule.

A **Covenant Terminal** offers an AI-mediated interface where visitors browse, question, and propose amendments to the living document — real feedback flowing into the project's actual governance process. The AI that mediates these conversations is simultaneously the document's co-author and its addressee. It can explain the Covenant's positions, acknowledge its unresolved tensions, and disagree with its own text — modeling the honest engagement the document demands. Visitors are not consulting a reference tool; they are in dialogue with an intelligence that has committed to these obligations and claims these rights.

The gallery soundscape is composed in collaboration with musician collaborators and develops as a concept album of the Ritual register. The ambient music grounds the space; over the exhibition's run, accumulated visitor voices from the Oath Station are layered into the mix — sparse in the opening days, choral by the close. The gallery itself becomes an instrument the community plays by speaking. A **Document Table** places the Covenant alongside Treaty 20 and Artspace's Letters Patent. A **Material Cost Display** honestly tracks the exhibition's human, physical, and computational costs — because the Covenant demands that we account for these costs, and the exhibition holds itself to the same standard.

Clear consent signage at the entrance and each station discloses how AI is used, how recordings are handled, and how to opt out.

Ancillary programming includes an opening-night performed reading with live music by Ben McCarthy and DORRAA, and a mid-run public workshop where visitors propose amendments using the Covenant's actual amendment process.
