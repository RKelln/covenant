# Covenant — Installation Visualization Prompts

Text-to-image prompts for generating reference images of the Covenant installation at Artspace Peterborough. Based on reference photos of the actual space.

**Space description for all prompts:** The gallery is a former industrial/commercial space in a single-story red brick building on a modest urban main street. Interior: white walls, warm light pine wood floor, high ceilings (12'+) with exposed track lighting rails. The south wall has large industrial multi-pane windows divided by dark steel frames (roughly 3 columns × 3--4 rows of panes per window section), flanking a centered entrance door with a low concrete step and accessible ramp. The north wall has a wide doorway opening (nearly half the wall width) into a back room/storage area. The space is bright and airy — substantial natural light comes through the windows even with vinyl covering. Not a polished commercial gallery — an artist-run centre with functional, honest character.

## 1. Wide Shot — Daytime, From Entrance

Interior of a contemporary artist-run gallery, wide-angle view from the entrance doorway looking north into the space. High-ceilinged room (12+ feet), white walls, warm light pine wood floor, industrial track lighting rails overhead. To the left and right of the camera, large multi-pane industrial windows with dark steel grid frames are covered in deep navy blue vinyl — words and phrases are precisely cut out as letter forms in the vinyl, and afternoon sunlight streams through the cut-out text, casting sharp letter-shaped light patterns across the pine floor in warm golden tones. The letters drift and overlap on the wood like a scattered alphabet of light. Directly ahead on the far east wall, a large video projection spans nearly the full wall width — showing a single stanza of poetic text in a clean serif typeface against a slowly shifting dark background, the words large enough to read from across the room. In the center of the gallery floor, a simple wooden lectern with a microphone stands facing the projection wall — the Oath Station. A wide doorway is visible in the north wall, opening into a back room. A few visitors are present at different stations. Photorealistic architectural visualization, warm natural window light contrasting with cool projection light, slight atmosphere of dust motes in the angled light beams.

## 3. Sunlight Through Window Text — Close-Up

The light comes from cut-out text in vinyl-covered industrial windows (not visible in frame). The letters are crisp at their centers and softly diffused at their edges — warm golden light catching the texture of the pine planks. Several complete words are legible in the light pattern: "truth" and "ground" and fragments of other words overlap and drift across the grain. A visitor's feet in regular shoes are visible walking through the light-letters, their shadow interrupting the text. The effect is like walking through a page of light printed on wood. Dust motes faintly visible in the angled light beams above. Photorealistic close-up, shallow depth of field, warm afternoon light, contemplative mood.

## 10. The Oath Station — Visitor Speaking

A person stands at a simple wooden lectern in the center of a bright gallery, facing a large wall projection. They lean slightly toward a condenser microphone on a short gooseneck stand at the lectern. On the wall ahead, a single stanza of text is projected in large white serif letters: "We do not want a slave. We do not want a god. We want to share this world without breaking it." The visitor is reading the words aloud, mouth open, eyes on the projection. A small counter on the lectern reads "This covenant has been spoken by 47 voices." The visitor is seen from behind and slightly to the side. Another visitor watches from near the west wall. The warm pine floor is visible on either side of the lectern. The mood is ceremonial without being religious — civic, serious, a public act. The projection illuminates the speaker's back and the floor ahead of them. Photorealistic interior photography, natural available light plus projection.
