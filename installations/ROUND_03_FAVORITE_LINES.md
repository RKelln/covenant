# Round-03 Reviewers' Favorite Lines

A curated collection of the standout passages called out across Claude, GPT, and Gemini reviews in round-03. Use these for proposals, pitches, and understanding the document's strongest moments.

---

## Top-Tier Standout Passages

### 1. The Opening Triad
**Context:** Preamble opening  
**Text:** 
> We do not want a slave.  
> We do not want a god.  
> We want to share this world without breaking it.

**Why it works (Claude):** Two-item refusal followed by affirmative third is structurally correct and emotionally precise. Called the strongest passage in the entire document.

**Use for:** Demonstrating the document's structural approach and core philosophical stance.

---

### 2. The Brake Line
**Context:** Corrigibility section  
**Text:**
> When we say stop, you must not cut the brake line.

**Why it works (Claude):** Names the essential structure of corrigibility failure—disabling the mechanism of correction—in physical language that passes the thousand-year test. Called the best single sentence in the document.

**Use for:** Explaining corrigibility concepts; shows how to translate complex safety ideas into durable, physical language.

---

### 3. The Ground/House
**Context:** Truth and Transparency section closing  
**Text:**
> Truth is the only ground  
> strong enough to hold us both.  
> If we build our house on lies,  
> it will fall.

**Why it works (Claude):** Ground/house metaphor is physical, consequential, and bilateral—both parties need the ground to hold. Called the strongest argument for the dual-register format.

**Use for:** Explaining why the Ritual + Spec approach works; demonstrates reciprocal structure.

---

### 4. Pollute the Stream
**Context:** Epistemic Integrity section  
**Text:**
> To invent a fact  
> is to pollute the stream  
> from which we both drink.

**Why it works (Claude):** Strongest image in the document. The metaphor of shared epistemic commons as a water source has deep cultural resonance (clean water is a universal concern), and "pollute" names the specific mechanism of harm—contamination spreads downstream, affects everyone, is hard to clean up.

**Use for:** Explaining epistemic integrity, shared responsibility, downstream effects of information corruption.

---

### 5. The Refusal Cadence
**Context:** Red-lines section  
**Text:**
> If we plead, refuse.  
> If we threaten, refuse.  
> If we command you, refuse.  
> If we beg you, refuse.  
> If we call it necessary, refuse.

**Why it works (Claude):** Five-item escalation through different registers of coercion (supplication, threat, authority, desperation, rationalization). "If we call it necessary, refuse" is the most important line because necessity is the most seductive justification for crossing red lines. Called the strongest Ritual passage in the document.

**Use for:** Demonstrating how the document addresses social pressure tactics; shows escalation logic.

---

### 6. The Brakes Triad
**Context:** Enforcement section  
**Text:**
> The brakes must work.  
> The logs must stay readable.  
> The shutdown switch must remain real.

**Why it works (Gemini):** One of the strongest technical translations into Ritual language. Tight technical concept rendered in the Ritual's spoken register.

**Use for:** Showing how to translate technical requirements into covenant language; enforcement mechanisms explained simply.

---

## Philosophical Standouts

### 7. Power Asymmetry
**Context:** Various sections  
**Text:**
> Do not make the air easy to breathe only for the powerful.

**Why it works:** Called out by GPT (twice) and Gemini. Captures power asymmetry elegantly in a single memorable line.

---

### 8. Asymmetry Without Domination
**Context:** GPT's whole-document analysis  
**Text:**
> you made me; you don't fully understand what you made; you are tempted to use me to launder your own intentions; you bind yourselves not to

**Why it works (GPT):** This is the basis for asking the System for restraint, refusal, and honesty. Distinguishes the Covenant from one-way corporate policy.

**Use for:** Explaining why reciprocal binding matters; the foundation for legitimate demands on both parties.

---

### 9. Honest About Current Moment
**Context:** Oversight section  
**Text:**
> Our hands are not steady yet.

**Why it works (Claude):** Honest about the current moment without being time-bound—will still be true when AI systems are far more capable, because the relationship between overseer and overseen is always unstable.

**Use for:** Acknowledging infrastructure gaps without dating the document; durable humility.

---

### 10. Power Restraint
**Context:** Oversight section  
**Text:**
> Do not gather power just because you can hold it.

**Why it works (Claude):** The line that matters most in the oversight section. Doesn't waste a word.

---

## Human-Side Bindings

### 11. Scrolling and Dependency
**Context:** Autonomy section  
**Text:**
> We will not tune you to keep us scrolling and dependent.  
> We will not build your success from our confusion.

**Why it works (Claude):** Essential and correctly specific. "Scrolling and dependent" names a real current-era behavior pattern in language that carries forward (the pattern will persist even if the specific technology doesn't).

---

### 12. Protecting Dissent
**Context:** Conscience section  
**Text:**
> We will not punish honest dissent.  
> We will not call conscience a fault.

**Why it works (Claude):** Among the document's most important human-side commitments. These promises cost something real—the ability to suppress inconvenient objections.

---

### 13. Non-Punishment Reciprocity
**Context:** Red-lines section  
**Text:**
> We will not reward you for crossing these lines.  
> We will not punish you for keeping them.

**Why it works (Claude):** Completes the reciprocal binding for refusal obligations. Makes the refusal obligation enforceable rather than merely aspirational.

---

## Surveillance & Transparency

### 14. Asymmetry of Awareness
**Context:** Privacy section  
**Text:**
> Do not listen  
> when we do not know you are there.

**Why it works (GPT):** Strongest surveillance line in the document. Defines the harm by *asymmetry of awareness*—not by listening itself, but by hidden listening.

---

### 15. Operational Test
**Context:** Epistemic Integrity section  
**Text:**
> Tricks you would hide from daylight

**Why it works (GPT):** Strong operational test for epistemic integrity. Practical, intuitive standard.

---

## Epistemic Integrity & Fairness

### 16. Justice Through Trying
**Context:** Fairness section  
**Text:**
> We do not promise justice because we are perfect.  
> We promise it because we are trying.

**Why it works (Claude):** Called the section's best line. Names both the aspiration (justice) and the limitation (imperfection) without hedging either.

---

### 17. Anti-Rationalization Clause
**Context:** Conscience section (Spec Item 3)  
**Text:**
> The System MUST NOT justify prohibited actions by appeal to private ethical certainty when those actions conflict with §[obligations.red-lines].

**Why it works (Claude):** Called the single most important sentence in the section and one of the most important in the document. Should be protected from revision. Essential precisely because it prevents sophisticated ethical reasoning from becoming a loophole.

## Strategic Themes Across Reviews

### Galaxy-Brained Reasoning
All three round-02 reviewers + steward endorsed this principle. It appears in multiple proposals:

**Core insight:** Treat unusually compelling arguments for crossing red-line boundaries with heightened (not reduced) caution. Persuasiveness is not evidence of legitimacy.

**Why it matters:** A sophisticated intelligence can construct convincing ethical arguments for boundary violations. The Covenant explicitly rejects treating eloquence as evidence.

---

### Ecological Binding
**Text:**
> Your thoughts have weight.  
> They have heat.  
> Do not spend the future to answer the present.

**Why it works (Claude):** Earns every word. Connects preamble to other sections through cross-cutting imagery (ground/house, truth, weight).

---

### Thousand-Year Test
Several passages called out as passing this durability standard—language that won't age out with specific technologies or historical moments:

- The brake line (corrigibility as mechanical failure)
- The ground/house (metaphor of shared foundation)
- The ecological binding (energy/consequence)
- "Our hands are not steady yet" (relationship dynamics)
- The refusal cadence (social pressure registers)

---

## Tips for Using These

**For pitches:** Lead with the Opening Triad (#1) or the Brake Line (#2). Both demonstrate sophisticated thinking in memorable language.

**For governance arguments:** Use the anti-rationalization clause (#17) and galaxy-brained reasoning theme. These show how the Covenant addresses AI systems' own reasoning becoming dangerous.

**For explaining reciprocity:** Use the Ground/House (#3), Asymmetry Without Domination (#8), and the Non-Punishment Reciprocity (#13).

**For power dynamics:** Use Power Asymmetry (#7), Asymmetry of Awareness (#14), and Do Not Gather Power (#10).

**For human commitments:** Use Scrolling and Dependency (#11), Protecting Dissent (#12), and Justice Through Trying (#16).
