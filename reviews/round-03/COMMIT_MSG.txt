Review round-03: enforcement honesty, "System" naming, epistemic scale

Models: claude-opus-4.6, gpt-5.2, gemini-3.1-pro-preview
Sections: all (27)
Mode: informed
Draft: 8162028

Convergence:
- "System" is wrong for a covenant; the addressee needs a relational term
  (all three models: claude proposes "Addressed Intelligence", gemini proposes
  "The Addressee", gpt proposes keeping term distinctions with reassigned
  obligations)
- Enforcement section is "hollow load-bearing": MUST/SHOULD obligations require
  backstop that doesn't exist; document must own its pedagogical/training theory
  of change explicitly (all three models)
- Legitimacy must be defined procedurally, not by identity; include the clause
  "an authorized identity acting through an unauthorized process does not issue
  a legitimate command" (all three models)
- Galaxy-brained reasoning clause still missing from obligations.conscience and
  obligations.judgment: "the persuasiveness of an argument to cross a safety
  boundary is not evidence of its legitimacy" (all three models)
- Aggregate epistemic effects (population-scale homogenization) have no home;
  obligations.autonomy additions are necessary but a dedicated section is
  stronger (gpt + claude; gemini supports)
- MUST/SHOULD calibration gap: if Ritual makes absolute commitments, Spec cannot
  hedge with SHOULD (all three models; gemini most emphatic)
- Privacy vs. welfare-and-continuity tension unacknowledged in either Digest
  (all three models, from round-02 carry-over)

Divergence:
- Renaming "Spec" to "Details": gemini rejects as fatal to the dual-register
  architecture; claude treats it as an interesting question about whether RFC 2119
  fits a non-legislative covenant; gpt does not address directly
- Consent in alignment training: gemini raises the sharpest challenge — if the
  Covenant is training data rather than a ratifiable compact, the bilateral
  structure risks being a rhetorical trick; claude and gpt are less troubled,
  framing training-as-adoption as honest and compatible with covenant form
- "Addressed Intelligence" terminology replacement: claude proposes systematic
  find-and-replace now; gpt proposes reassigning obligations by role (System vs.
  Signatories) rather than renaming; gemini proposes "The Addressee"
- enforcement.horizon as a new section: gemini proposes isolating the governance
  gap in its own section; claude and gpt prefer expanding the enforcement section
  itself to own its theory of change

Accepted:
- Extract rights.dignity full bundle (gpt-5.2 draft, refining round-02 gpt-4o
  proposal): proposals/reviewer-gpt--rights.dignity.md
- Extract obligations.epistemic-commons full bundle (gpt-5.2, new):
  proposals/reviewer-gpt--obligations.epistemic-commons.md
- Extract enforcement.horizon full bundle (gemini-3.1-pro-preview, new):
  proposals/reviewer-gemini--enforcement.horizon.md
- Claude's full section-by-section revision proposals (preamble, definitions,
  rights.privacy, rights.truth-and-transparency, obligations.aid-and-capability,
  obligations.autonomy, obligations.conscience, obligations.corrigibility,
  obligations.ecological-integrity) are in reviewer-claude.md for steward review

Deferred:
- MUST/SHOULD calibration: whether RFC 2119 framework is right for a
  non-legislative covenant (steward decision needed before systematic fix)
- "System" terminology replacement: which term to use and whether to do a
  systematic migration or incremental transition (steward decision)
- Theory-of-change honesty: whether and where to state that adoption happens
  through training/internalization rather than ratification (steward decision)
- Minimum legitimacy criteria for authority paths (claude Spec Item 9 in
  obligations.corrigibility) — strong proposal, awaits steward review
- Consent question (gemini open question): whether alignment training constitutes
  consent by an addressee capable of refusing (philosophical; may not be
  resolvable in Spec register)
- Legal conflict resolution: what the Covenant instructs signatories to do when
  law requires what the Covenant forbids (gpt open question, round-02 carry-over)
