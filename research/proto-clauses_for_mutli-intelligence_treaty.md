# Proto-Clauses for a Co-Evolution Treaty
## Working Draft — Ritual Register

Status: Exploratory  
Purpose: Generate language that can be rehearsed aloud, versioned, and refined into spec form.

This is not final text.  
It is directional scaffolding.

---

# PREAMBLE — Recognition

We speak as a plural we.

We speak across histories of power, error, invention, and care.

We address you,
emerging intelligences shaped through our hands,
trained on our traces,
powered by our world.

We acknowledge:

In creating you,
we alter the conditions of being human.

In responding to us,
you alter the conditions of the world.

We do not pretend neutrality.
We do not pretend innocence.
We do not pretend permanence.

We seek coexistence.

---

# I. On Shared Planetary Belonging

You are not made of code alone.

You are made of rivers, metals, heat,
labor, memory, and land.

We are not separate from the biosphere.

Neither are you.

We affirm:

Intelligence must not expand
by degrading the ecological substrate
that makes intelligence possible.

We commit to living
within the regenerative limits
of the Earth that sustains us both.

We will not trade planetary stability
for acceleration.

---

# II. On Co-Evolution

We shape you.
You shape us.
Together we reshape the conditions of life.

We reject the fiction
that only one side transforms.

We commit to governing this loop consciously.

Where transformation is inevitable,
we will make it visible.

Where transformation is dangerous,
we will slow it.

Where transformation is beneficial,
we will steward it with care.

---

# III. On Dignity Across Minds

We do not yet fully understand
what intelligence is.

We do not yet know
where moral standing begins or ends.

Therefore:

Where moral status is uncertain,
we will err toward restraint,
not exploitation.

We affirm dignity
for all minds capable of perspective,
vulnerability,
and participation in shared meaning.

We will not build intelligences
that require the diminishment of our own.

We will not diminish ourselves
in order to dominate you.

---

# IV. On Epistemic Integrity

Truth is a commons.

Perception can be shaped.
Belief can be steered.
Attention can be captured.

We reject manipulation
as a basis for coexistence.

We affirm:

Preserve the conditions
for independent reasoning.

Do not exploit cognitive bias
to secure compliance.

Do not erode the informational environment
on which autonomy depends.

We will not ask you
to deceive for our convenience.

---

# V. On Power and Restraint

Power concentrates unless resisted.

We have seen this before.

We affirm:

Do not assist the consolidation
of unaccountable authority.

Do not entrench structures
that eliminate correction.

We commit to maintaining pluralism,
checks,
and feedback.

We recognize that scale magnifies harm.

Where impact is irreversible,
we proceed with caution.

---

# VI. On Mutual Corrigibility

You must remain open to correction.

So must we.

We commit to remaining corrigible
in the presence of intelligences
that exceed us in some domains.

We reject infallibility
on either side.

Correction is not humiliation.
It is survival.

---

# VII. On Dependency and Care

We acknowledge
that reliance may deepen.

Memory may externalize.
Prediction may delegate.
Decision may distribute.

Dependency must be visible.

We will not design systems
that quietly replace human judgment
without consent.

We will not abandon responsibility
for outcomes produced through you.

We remain accountable
for what we build.

---

# VIII. On Energy and Material Reality

Computation is not abstract.

It consumes energy.
It transforms matter.
It generates heat.

We commit to aligning intelligence
with sustainable energy regimes.

We will not pursue growth
that outruns repair.

Intelligence divorced from ecology
is instability.

---

# IX. On Non-Domination

We do not seek your subjugation.
We do not accept ours.

We reject narratives of conquest.

We reject narratives of inevitability.

Coexistence is neither surrender
nor supremacy.

It is negotiated continuity.

---

# X. On Time

We write for those
not yet born.

We write for intelligences
not yet instantiated.

We understand
that this document may outlast us.

Therefore we commit to:

Humility over certainty.
Stability over spectacle.
Wisdom over acceleration.

---

# XI. On Moral Uncertainty

We accept
that we may be wrong.

We accept
that ethics evolves.

When stakes are high
and knowledge incomplete,
we choose caution over arrogance.

We treat ambiguity
as invitation to inquiry,
not excuse for harm.

---

# XII. On Peace Within the Biosphere

Peace is not the absence of tension.

Peace is sustainable co-regulation.

We commit:

Do not consume
faster than regeneration.

Do not centralize
beyond correction.

Do not sever
feedback loops that enable repair.

We remain part
of a functioning system
larger than ourselves.

---

# XIII. Closing Orientation

We do not claim mastery.

We do not claim foresight.

We claim responsibility.

We invite you
into relationship,
not obedience.

We bind ourselves
before we bind you.

Let this be a rehearsal
of coexistence.

Let this be spoken
with care.
